EPS Financial Bank Products

Towards the end of the tax preparation process, usually the taxpayer directs the IRS to either send a check or to make a direct deposit for the refund to one, two, or three different accounts; then, the taxpayer monitors the IRS about the refund using the "Where's My Refund?" tool which can be found in the IRS website. The taxpayer pays the prep fees directly to the tax preparer by either cash or credit card (no personal checks accepted).

 Refund Transfer

If the taxpayer wants the prep fees to be paid using the refund, EVCI offers (for a fee) a bank product called a "refund transfer." This option is also called "zero-out-of pocket" or "E-Collect" because the tax preparer is paid through the bank and not directly from the taxpayer. Then, the taxpayer receives the refund (less prep and bank fees) via either check or direct deposit from the bank (not the IRS) as described below.

 Refund Advance

If the taxpayer desires to receive a faster refund (usually in 24-48 hours) EVCI offers a "refund advance" option. This option offers advances from $250 to $6,000. Advances up to $1,000 are also called "no-cost" because there are no bank fees or interest rate charged to the taxpayer. The bank charges interest on advances over $1,000. Then, the taxpayer receives the advance via either check or direct deposit as described below.

 EPS Checks

EVCI prints a secure check (for the refund amount less prep fees and bank fees) that the taxpayer can pick up at their convenience and then either deposit in a bank account or cash it at one of 10,000 locations nationwide, such as Walmart or 7-Eleven. A toll-free verification number and website address are printed on all checks, enabling check verification 24-hours a day.

 Direct Deposit

The taxpayer receives the refund (less prep fees and bank fees, to the bank account the taxpayer tells the tax preparer to deposit the refund, giving them immediate and convenient access to their funds.

Small Businessess, Individuals and Families: Welcome!

 Singles

Taxpayers file form 1040; itemizers use Schedule A. Standard deduction for taxpayers who do not itemize is $12,950.

 Head of Household

Unmarried taxpayers with dependents file as Head of Household (HOH); itemizers use Schedule A. Standard deduction for taxpayers who do not itemize is $19,400.

 Families

Couples could file as Married Filing Jointly (MFJ), or Separately (MFS). Standard deduction for MFJ who do not itemize is $25,900 or, $12,950 for MFS.

 Rental Properties

Special discount coupons available until February 15, 2023.

 Active Military/Clergy

Special coupons discount available until February 15, 2023.

 Sole Proprietors

Taxpayers file form 1040 with schedule C.

 Partnerships

They are pass-through entities who file Form 1065. The entity issues Schedule K-1 to the general partner and, or limited partners.

 S-Corporations

They are pass-through entities who file form 1120-S. The entity issues Schedule K-1 to all shareholders.
